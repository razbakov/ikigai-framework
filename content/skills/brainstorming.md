# Brainstorming Session

This skill facilitates structured brainstorming when you're facing a decision, starting something new, or feeling stuck. Instead of jumping straight to solutions, it forces you to explore the problem space first — mapping constraints, generating diverse options, and then converging on the strongest approach.

Most solo founders skip brainstorming because there's no one to brainstorm with. This skill fills that gap with a structured process that prevents both analysis paralysis and premature commitment.

## How It Works

1. **Define the intent** — What are you trying to achieve? The agent clarifies the goal, target audience, and success criteria before generating any ideas.
2. **Map constraints** — Identify hard constraints (budget, time, tech stack) and soft constraints (preferences, assumptions). Constraints shape the solution space.
3. **Diverge** — Generate a wide range of options without judging them. The agent plays different perspectives: user, competitor, minimalist, maximalist. Quantity over quality at this stage.
4. **Evaluate** — Score each option against the defined constraints and success criteria. Identify trade-offs explicitly. No option is perfect — the question is which trade-offs you can live with.
5. **Converge** — Select the strongest option (or combine elements from multiple options) and articulate why it wins. Document the decision and the alternatives considered.

## When to Use

- Before starting a new feature or project — explore before committing
- When you're stuck between multiple approaches
- When a decision feels too complex to make alone
- During quarterly planning to evaluate which projects deserve continued investment
- When you catch yourself defaulting to the first idea that comes to mind

## Requirements

- No integrations required — this is a conversational skill
- A clear question or decision to brainstorm about
- Openness to exploring options you might not have considered
- 15-30 minutes of focused time
